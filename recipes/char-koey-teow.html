char kway teow recipes

Home

ingredients

  • 300g fresh flat rice noodles (kway teow) (or dried rice noodles, soaked and drained)
  • 150g prawns (peeled and deveined)
  • 100g bean sprouts
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 Chinese sausage (lap cheong), thinly sliced (optional)
  • 100g chives, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 6-8 cockles (kerang), cleaned (optional)
  • 1 tsp chili paste (sambal) (optional for extra spice)

Sauces

  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce (for color and richness)
  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce (for saltiness)
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp fish sauce
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp white pepper
  • 1 tbsp water

steps

  1. Prepare the Sauce:

    • Mix all the sauce ingredients in a bowl and set aside.

  2. Heat the Wok:

    • Use a well-seasoned wok or non-stick pan.
    • Add 1 tbsp oil and heat until smoking.

  3. Cook the Prawns & Sausage:

    • Add prawns and Chinese sausage (if using) and stir-fry for about 1 minute until prawns turn pink.
    • Remove and set aside.

  4. Sauté Garlic & Noodles:

    • Add the remaining 1 tbsp oil to the wok.
    • Toss in minced garlic and stir-fry for 10 seconds until fragrant.
    • Add kway teow noodles and stir-fry quickly.

  5. Add Sauce & Stir-Fry:

    • Pour in the prepared sauce and toss the noodles well to coat them evenly.
    • Push the noodles to the side.

  6. Cook the Eggs:

    • Crack in the eggs and scramble slightly before mixing them with the noodles.

  7. Add Prawns & Cockles:

    • Return the cooked prawns and add the cockles (if using).
    • Stir-fry for another 30 seconds.

  8. Final Touch:

    • Toss in bean sprouts and chives.
    • Stir-fry for another 30 seconds. The sprouts should remain crunchy.

  9. Serve Immediately:

    • Dish out while hot and serve with lime wedges or sambal on the side.
